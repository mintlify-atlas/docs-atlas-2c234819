SGR-Turnos (Sistema de Gestión y Reserva de Turnos) is an internal web application built with **Symfony 8** and **PHP 8.4** that replaces the physical queue at bank branch ATMs with a structured, identity-verified reservation system. It stores data in **MySQL 8.0** (or MariaDB 10.5+) and renders all screens with **Twig** templates styled using Tailwind CSS. The system runs entirely on a local area network and requires no internet connection once deployed.

## The Problem

In many bank branches, a practice known locally as _colerismo_ undermines fair access to ATM services. Individuals arrive before opening hours to occupy the first positions in line, then sell those positions to other customers. A related problem is cash hoarding: the same person withdraws the daily maximum on consecutive days, acting as an informal intermediary and depleting the ATM for everyone else.

These problems share a root cause — the physical queue has no memory and no identity link. There is no way to know who is in line, whether they withdrew yesterday, or whether they purchased their spot.

| Problem | Operational consequence |
|---|---|
| **Turn reselling** | Individuals accumulate the first positions and sell them, creating disorder and unlawful profit. |
| **Cash hoarding** | The same person withdraws the maximum nearly every day, acting as an intermediary and leaving the ATM empty for others. |
| **No traceability** | There is no prior record of who will withdraw money or when their last withdrawal was. |

## What SGR-Turnos Does

SGR-Turnos substitutes the disordered physical queue with a **virtual appointment calendar**, enforcing three core controls:

- **Identity-linked turns** — every reservation is bound to a national identity number (CI). A turn cannot exist without an identified person behind it, eliminating anonymous queue reselling.
- **Frequency control** — the system enforces a configurable lockout period (default: 7 days) per identity number. A customer who has already withdrawn cannot reserve a new turn until the lockout expires.
- **Audit trail** — every action in the system is logged with a timestamp, the responsible user, before/after data snapshots, and an anonymized IP address (SHA-256 hash with subnet masking).
