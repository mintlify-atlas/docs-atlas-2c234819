SGR-Turnos is a web application designed for bank branches that need to manage ATM and teller queues fairly and transparently. Instead of physical first-come-first-served lines where positions are bought and sold, the system issues numbered turns tied to each customer's national identity card (CI), applies configurable blocking periods to prevent daily withdrawals by the same person, and gives branch staff a printable daily roster and full audit trail.

The system runs entirely on a local network — no internet connection is required — and can be deployed on low-cost or recycled hardware with Apache or Nginx.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Client enters their identity card number">
    At the public terminal (`/ingreso`), the customer types their 11-digit Cuban CI. The system validates format and birth-date, then checks whether the client is within a blocking period from a recent withdrawal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client selects a service and available date">
    Unblocked clients choose a service (ATM, teller window, etc.) and a date that still has available quota. The calendar only shows dates that have been configured and still have open slots.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client confirms and receives a printed ticket">
    After providing name, phone number, and (where applicable) the withdrawal amount, the system assigns a unique turn number and presents a print-ready ticket.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff marks turns as used at end of day">
    An admin or supervisor marks completed turns as **USADO** from the daily turn list. This automatically starts the client's blocking period, preventing another withdrawal before the configured number of days has passed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  SGR-Turnos operates fully offline on a local area network (LAN). No cloud account or internet connection is needed during normal operation.
</Note>
